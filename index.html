8 Key Factors That Determine Website Price

Before looking at packages, it's important to understand what really drives the cost of a website. These eight factors explain why one website costs Rs 40,000 and another goes beyond Rs 300,000.

1

Website Type & Technology Stack

The first and biggest factor is the type of website you need. The technology stack directly affects cost.

Standard Development

Ideal for startups and service businesses that need a professional presence.

Custom Code Development

When you need unique functionality, our custom solutions are built using modern frameworks.

2

Features & Functionalities

Every additional feature takes time to design, build, and test. A simple brochure site is cheaper than a complex system.

  • Online store setup & product management
  • Secure payment gateways (PayHere, PayPal)
  • Appointment booking & forms
  • Membership & restricted areas
  • Custom dashboards
3

Design Quality & User Experience

High-quality, conversion-focused design requires more expertise. Custom layouts, smooth animations, and consistent branding increase performance and trust dramatically.

4

SEO & Content Quality

Proper SEO setup helps your pages rank on Google. Projects include core SEO essentials, technical optimization, metadata, and clean site structure.

5

Hosting, Domain & Security

Cheap hosting causes slow loading times and downtime. Quality hosting, secure SSL certificates, and a .com domain form the solid foundation of your website.

6

Mobile Responsiveness

More than 90% of visitors browse using their phones. Every project is fully responsive, adapting seamlessly to mobiles, tablets, and desktops.

7

Developer Expertise & Support

Working with an experienced freelance developer gives you direct communication, accountability, long-term stability, and dedicated technical support.

8

Ongoing Maintenance & Updates

A website is a living system. It needs regular updates, backups, performance checks, and security monitoring to stay fast and secure over time.

Admin Roles and Access Control

Who is the admin?

The developer holds Super Admin access. The client gets a Client Admin role.

Role Breakdown

Access Level Developer Client
Source code Full access No access
Server/hosting Full access No access
Database Full access No access
Domain/DNS Full access View only (or shared)
Content editing Full access Full access
Media uploads Full access Full access
User management Full access Limited (own staff only)
Core system & dependency updates Full access No access
Analytics Full access View only
Payment gateway settings Full access View only
Backups Full access No access

Why this matters

  • Developer maintains control over the codebase and infrastructure
  • Client can update their own text, images, and basic content
  • Prevents accidental changes that could break the site
  • If the client wants full ownership, everything can be transferred at project handoff (handover fee applies)
