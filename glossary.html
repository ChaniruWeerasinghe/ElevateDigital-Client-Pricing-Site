Glossary of Terms

Plain-English explanations for the technical terms used in our pricing and packages.

CMS (Content Management System)

A software application that allows you to easily manage, create, and modify digital content on your website without needing to write code (e.g., WordPress, Shopify, Sanity).

SEO (Search Engine Optimization)

The practice of improving your website to increase its visibility when people search for products or services related to your business on Google or other search engines.

Domain & DNS

Your Domain is your website's address on the internet (like www.yourbusiness.com). DNS (Domain Name System) is like the internet's phonebook, pointing your domain to the server where your website lives.

SSL Certificate

A digital certificate that authenticates a website's identity and enables an encrypted connection. It's what gives your website the "HTTPS" and the little padlock icon in the browser, keeping user data secure.

Hosting / Server

The physical or cloud-based computers where your website's files, images, and code are stored so they can be accessed over the internet 24/7.

Meta Tags & Open Graph

Meta Tags are hidden text snippets in the code that tell search engines what a page is about. Open Graph tags ensure your website looks good (with an image, title, and description) when shared on social media platforms like Facebook or WhatsApp.

Sitemap

A file that lists all the important pages on your website. It acts as a roadmap for search engines like Google, helping them find and index your content faster.

Schema Markup

Special code added to your website that helps search engines provide more informative results for users (like showing star ratings, prices, or event dates directly in Google search results).

Responsive Design

An approach to web design that ensures your website looks great and works perfectly on all devices—whether it's a large desktop monitor, a tablet, or a mobile phone.

Lightbox Gallery

A feature where clicking on an image expands it to fill the screen, dimming the rest of the webpage in the background so the user can view the photo without distraction.

CRM (Customer Relationship Management)

Software used to manage interactions with current and potential customers. Integrating a CRM with your website means leads from forms automatically flow into your sales system.

CDN (Content Delivery Network)

A network of servers distributed globally. When a user visits your site, the CDN serves the site from the server closest to them, dramatically speeding up load times.

CAPTCHA

A security feature used on forms to determine whether the user is human or a spam bot, protecting your inbox from automated junk mail.

GDPR & Cookie Consent

GDPR is a strict privacy law. A Cookie Consent Banner is a popup that asks users for permission to track their data, which is legally required in many regions if you use analytics or ads.

API (Application Programming Interface)

A software intermediary that allows two different applications to talk to each other. For example, using an API allows your website to pull real-time shipping rates from a courier service.

ICANN Fee

A small, mandatory annual fee charged by the Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers when registering or renewing a domain name.

E-Commerce & Payment Gateways

E-Commerce refers to buying and selling online. A Payment Gateway (like Stripe, PayPal, or PayHere) is the secure technology that reads and processes credit card transactions on your store.
